Zoey Zhao. Preprint, 2026.
Advisor: Professor Zhu

In this project, I establish an elementary proof of the Łojasiewicz inequality for cubic polynomials in dimension two and obtain an improved exponent β through case by case analysis.

Ongoing Research

Computing Abelian Centralizers in Transitive Permutation Groups

Zoey Zhao. Ongoing project, 2026.
Project proposed by Yuxiang Yao, Ph.D. student and course instructor at UC Irvine.
Independent implementation, computations, and draft writing by Zoey Zhao.

This project computes the ratio Ad/Nd of transitive subgroups G ≤ Sd whose centralizer CSd(G) is abelian.

Using SageMath, GAP, and an independent SymPy verifier, I compute the data for 4 ≤ d ≤ 30 and identify two mechanisms behind the nonabelian exceptions: regular groups and stabilizer-normalizer cases.

Interdisciplinary Research

Loneliness as Social Reward Prediction Error: A Developmental Neurocognitive Framework of Adolescent Reward Recalibration

Zoey Zhao. Originally researched in 2023， substantially revised in 2026 following further academic study.
Advisor Prof. Robin A. Murphy

The paper proposes a conceptual framework interpreting loneliness as a form of social reward prediction error during adolescence. Rather than presenting empirical findings, the work synthesizes existing literature to explore how subjective social experience may interact with reward-learning mechanisms.
